// about

I'm a first-year PhD student in Social Psychology at Northwestern University, working with Dr. Daniel Molden. Before I knew what social psychology was, I was sending my friends Google Forms to rate hypothetical scenarios and posting Instagram polls to see what people thought of the random questions I asked!

Those questions didn't go away — they just became professional. I'm now interested in how our beliefs, behaviors, and sense of self shape the way we perceive and judge others. Along the way, two years running studies at Ohio State in the Motivation and Cognition Lab and a summer doing group counseling at an addiction treatment clinic gave me a pretty grounded sense of what that actually means.

Now, I run experiments to professionally find out what people think of my random questions. Scroll below to find out more about my work and interests. Thanks for visiting!

// research

01
Self-Control Strategies & Interpersonal Impressions

How does using abstinence vs. moderation as a self-control strategy shape how others see you? Preliminary findings reveal a competence-warmth tradeoff — abstinence boosts perceived competence while moderation enhances warmth.

collecting & analyzing data!
02
Bias Confrontation as a Goal-Conflict Dilemma

Investigating how competing goals influence whether people decide to confront bias. Full study lifecycle from design through data collection and thematic coding of confrontation narratives.

data analysis underway

// otherwise

developing questions: not yet hypotheses :P

Influencers & identity online

How does creating content for an audience shape your own sense of self — your beliefs, your values, the way you see yourself?

Cross-cultural definitions of success

How do cultural backgrounds shape what we think success looks like — and what happens when those definitions clash or get imposed across cultures?

Moral signals in close relationships

How do we choose who we want in our lives — romantically and platonically? What moral cues and self-beliefs guide who we're drawn to, and what we decide actually matters in those bonds?
