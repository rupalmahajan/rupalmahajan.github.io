// about

Hello! I'm a Ph.D. student in Social Psychology at Northwestern University, working with Dr. Daniel Molden. I'm drawn to questions about what we think and understand about people and ourselves. Before I knew what social psychology was, I was posting Instagram polls to see what people thought of the random questions I asked! Turns out I've always been interested in the why.

Two years running studies at The Ohio State University in the Motivation and Cognition Lab and a summer doing group counseling at an addiction treatment clinic showed me that there are still so many unanswered questions about human behavior.

Now, I run studies to experimentally find out what people think of my questions, with data to back it up! Keep scrolling to find out more about my work and interests, and feel free to reach out if anything sparks a thought. I love a good conversation!

01
collecting & analyzing data!
Self-Control Strategies & Interpersonal Impressions

How do the ways we approach self-control shape how others see us?

When someone restrains entirely versus allows themselves intentional balance, we judge them differently: how competent and warm they seem, whether we want to be around them, and how moral we think they are. And those strategies and the impressions they create look different across situations.

Bias Confrontation as a Goal-Conflict Dilemma

When do we speak up against bias — and when do we stay silent?

Competing goals shape whether people decide to confront bias at all, and different situations and types of bias create different pressures, motivations, and reasons for acting or holding back.

// otherwise

developing questions: not yet hypotheses :P

Influencers & identity online

How does creating content for an audience shape your own sense of self — your beliefs, your values, the way you see yourself?

Moral norms & technology use online

How do communities decide what "healthy" tech use looks like? How do those norms spread, even when they diverge from how people actually behave?

Cross-cultural definitions of success

How do cultural backgrounds shape what we think success looks like — and what happens when those definitions clash or get imposed across cultures?

Moral signals in close relationships

How do we choose who we want in our lives — romantically and platonically? What moral cues and self-beliefs guide who we're drawn to, and what we decide actually matters in those bonds?
